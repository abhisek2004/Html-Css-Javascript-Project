Maheswar Panda was born on March 22, 1948, in Baragadia, Kalinganagar, Jajapur, Odisha, with the address 755026. He was the son of Late Bansidhar Panda and was married to Annapurna Panda in the year 1970. The couple had three children - two sons named Amaresh Panda and Amitav Panda, and a daughter named Itishree Panda. Maheswar Panda's life journey was marked by significant milestones and achievements. He passed his 10th-grade examinations in 1965 and completed his 12th-grade in 1967. Subsequently, he pursued his Bachelor of Arts (B.A) degree, which he successfully earned in 1973. His dedication to education led him to attain a Bachelor of Education (B.Ed) degree in 1977, equipping him with the qualifications to work as an educator. In 1985, Maheswar Panda completed a Diploma in Academic Supervision and School Management (ASSM). With this educational background, he ventured into the field of teaching, focusing on the Odia language. His career in education spanned several institutions, and his work history is as follows: 1. Hatibari High School - 1971. 2. Jabara High School - December 1971 to February 1972. 3. Brahmabarada High School - 1972 to 1978. 4. Shramashakti Ucha Bidyapith - Headmaster from March 1978 to September 1992. 5. Hingula High School, Baragadia - Headmaster from 1992 to March 2006. During his educational career, Maheswar Panda also ventured into administrative roles, contributing to the community: 1. Sukinda Block Ossta President (JAJPUR) - Served from 2000 to 2003. 2. Zilla Ossta (President) - Held the position from 2004 to 2006. In April 2006, Maheswar Panda embarked on a spiritual journey as the main pujak (priest) at the Kapileswar Shiv Temple in Duburi, Jajpur, Odisha. As the head priest at this revered place of worship, he played a crucial role in conducting religious ceremonies and rituals, taking care of the temple, and providing spiritual guidance to the devotees. Maheswar Panda's tenure as the main pujak was marked by his unwavering devotion to Lord Shiva, and his role was highly regarded by the community. He became known for his dedication to the god, and his service to the temple was exemplary. His contributions to religious and spiritual life in the region were valued by those who sought solace and guidance in the temple. Sadly, Maheswar Panda passed away on November 11, 2021 beacuse of , leaving behind a legacy of devotion, education, and community service. He was not only a dedicated educator and administrator but also a respected spiritual leader, making a lasting impact on the lives of the people he served. His journey from a teacher to a revered temple priest exemplifies a life dedicated to learning, service, and faith.

Legacy

Maheswar Panda, an esteemed educator and a beacon of wisdom, dedicated over three decades to shaping young minds in Odisha, India. From his humble beginnings at Hatibari High School in 1971, he embarked on a remarkable journey, leaving an indelible mark as Headmaster at Hingula High School,Baragadia. Beyond the classroom, he championed local governance, serving as President of Sukinda Block Ossta and Zilla Ossta, contributing to the betterment of his community. Born on March 22, 1948, and departing on November 11, 2021, Maheswar Panda's legacy as a teacher and a community leader will forever shine in the hearts of those he touched.
